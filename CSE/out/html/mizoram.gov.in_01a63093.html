Mizoram governance operates under a democratic framework with a unicameral legislature, the Mizoram Legislative Assembly, comprising 40 members elected for a five-year term. The executive branch is led by the Chief Minister, while the judiciary functions under the jurisdiction of the Gauhati High Court. The economy is primarily agrarian, with significant efforts to promote tourism, handloom, and handicrafts. Despite its challenging terrain, Mizoram has achieved substantial infrastructure development, prioritizing sustainable growth and progress.

Mystical Mizoram

A Paradise for everyone

Mizoram, situated in the northeastern region of India and bordered by Myanmar, Bangladesh, and other Indian states, is renowned for its picturesque landscapes and rich cultural heritage. The state capital, Aizawl, is also its largest city. Covering approximately 21,081 square kilometers, Mizoram has a population of around 1.1 million, predominantly consisting of various Mizo tribes. The state's culture is deeply intertwined with tribal traditions, marked by vibrant festivals such as Chapchar Kut, Mim Kut, and Pawl Kut. Mizo serves as the official language, and Christianity is the predominant religion.
